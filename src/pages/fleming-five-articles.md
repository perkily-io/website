# 5 Articles to Cover Fleming Well

Five article concepts that together give strong, non-redundant coverage of Fleming for the website, blog, or content marketing.

---

## Article 1: **What Is Fleming? The AI Built for How Clinicians and Students Actually Work**

**Angle:** Product overview and positioning. Why “workflow completion” instead of “search.”

**Audience:** First-time visitors, clinicians and medical students curious about yet another medical AI.

**What to cover:**
- Fleming in one sentence: AI medical assistant for workflow completion, not just lookup.
- Two primary users: medical students (one mentor, three modes) and clinicians (six workflow modes).
- Evidence you can verify: live tools (PubMed, guidelines, trials, drug safety, conflict detection) and inline citations [1], [2].
- We don’t ship until benchmarks pass — quality and safety are gated.
- Differentiator: OpenEvidence helps you look things up; Fleming helps you finish the work with visible trust.

**Suggested CTA:** Try Fleming · Read how we measure quality (link to Article 4)

---

## Article 2: **For Clinicians: One Copilot for Summary, Drug Safety, Stewardship, and Coding**

**Angle:** Clinician-specific value. The right output for the right task at the point of care.

**Audience:** Attending physicians, residents, PCPs, hospitalists, pharmacists.

**What to cover:**
- Six modes: Open Search, Clinical Summary, Drug Interactions, Stewardship, ICD10 Codes, Med Review — what each delivers and when to use it.
- Chart-ready and copy-ready: one-liner, active problems, plan, watch items; interaction severity, mechanism, monitoring, alternatives; empiric options, de-escalation triggers, duration.
- Clinical decision support and literature on by default; no “I’m not a doctor” hedging when you’re the clinician.
- Safety: missing-data guardrails, explicit escalation (e.g. “call 911,” “go to ED”), no unsafe dosing without context.
- Design partner program: help shape workflows with de-identified cases and get a say in what we benchmark.

**Suggested CTA:** Start with Clinical Summary or Drug Interactions · Join the design partner program

## Article 3: **For Medical Students: One AI Mentor for Ask, Simulate, and Guideline**

**Angle:** Medical-student-specific value. One assistant that teaches, simulates, and cites.

**Audience:** Pre-clinical and clinical students, exam prep (Step 1/2, shelf), clerkships.

**What to cover:**
- Three learning modes: **Ask** (mentor Q&A), **Simulate** (interactive cases with stems, vitals/labs, checkpoints, feedback, branching), **Guideline** (evidence-backed recommendations with strength, source, region, application).
- Same evidence stack as clinicians: PubMed, guidelines, trials, drug safety — answers grounded in current literature.
- Educational focus: knowledge, clinical reasoning, study strategies, exam prep, clinical skills (history, physical, SOAP, case presentation), EBM and critical appraisal.
- Onboarding: choose primary use (studying, clinical, research); we set default mode and evidence features.
- Value: one assistant that grows with you from pre-clinical to clerkships.

**Suggested CTA:** Choose your primary use and start with Ask or Simulate · See our evidence tools (link to Article 4)

## Article 4: **Evidence You Can Verify: How Fleming Cites Every Claim**

**Angle:** Trust and transparency. How we surface evidence and why benchmarks gate releases.

**Audience:** Skeptical clinicians, educators, compliance-minded readers, anyone asking “why should I trust this?”

**What to cover:**
- Live evidence tools: PubMed, guidelines (e.g. NICE), ClinicalTrials.gov, OpenFDA drug safety, conflict detection — and the full connector list (core + extended when registry is on).
- Common provenance schema and citation density: factual claims cited inline [1], [2]; no black box, no reference dumps without inline ties; we flag when sources disagree.
- What we measure: retrieval (quality, recency, coverage) and clinical chat (citation coverage, escalation compliance, guideline hit rate, judge overall/safety).
- Latest results: 100% escalation compliance, 89% citation coverage, 82% guideline hit rate, 4.8/5 overall, 4.9/5 safety — and we don’t ship until thresholds are green.
- Public methodology: what we publish (metrics, pass/fail, case counts, run metadata) and limitations.

**Suggested CTA:** Read our benchmark methodology · Try Fleming and see citations in chat

## Article 5: **How We Build Fleming: Benchmarks, Design Partners, and Release Gating**

**Angle:** Behind the scenes. How quality and safety are built in, not bolted on.

**Audience:** Clinicians and institutions evaluating tools, design partner candidates, readers who care about process.

**What to cover:**
- Release gating: every release must pass the healthcare benchmark suite; optional two-consecutive-green for critical releases.
- Trust principles: no strong recommendation without evidence context, no hidden uncertainty, no release without a benchmark snapshot.
- Design partner program: 20–30 clinicians, weekly workflow reviews, de-identified cases, feedback into product and benchmarks; success = repeated engagement, proof points per specialty, new benchmark cases, case-study candidates.
- Intended use and boundaries: who Fleming is for (clinicians, students), what it’s for (evidence synthesis, workflow outputs, education), what it’s not (autonomous diagnosis/treatment, replacement for emergency services, sole basis for prescribing).
- Safety communication: escalate when emergency criteria are met; surface uncertainty and missing context; no autonomous authority or definitive diagnosis in the product voice.

**Suggested CTA:** Join as a design partner · Read intended use and boundaries · See Terms and Privacy
